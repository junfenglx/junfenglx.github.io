hierarchical softmax¶

(1) $$ p(w | w_I) = \prod_{j=1}^{L(w)-1} \sigma \left( [\![ n(w, j+1) = ch(n(w, j)) ]\!] \cdot {v'_{n(w, j)}}^T v_{w_I} \right) $$

the hierarchical softmax gradient¶

First let

$$ hs_j = [\![ n(w, j+1) = ch(n(w, j)) ]\!] \cdot {v'_{n(w, j)}}^T v_{w_I} $$

And

$$ \frac {d hs_j} {v'_{n(w, j)}} = [\![ n(w, j+1) = ch(n(w, j)) ]\!] \cdot v_{w_I} $$$$ \frac {d hs_j} {v_{w_I}} = [\![ n(w, j+1) = ch(n(w, j)) ]\!] \cdot v'_{n(w, j)} $$

Then $$ \nabla \log p(w | w_I) = \sum_{j=1}^{L(w)-1} \frac{1} {\sigma (hs_j)} \cdot \sigma' (hs_j) \cdot hs'_j $$

According to equation (0) $$ \nabla \log p(w | w_I) = \sum_{j=1}^{L(w)-1} \frac{1} {\sigma (hs_j)} \cdot \sigma (hs_j) \cdot [ 1 - \sigma (hs_j) ] \cdot hs'_j $$

simplify $$ \nabla \log p(w | w_I) = \sum_{j=1}^{L(w)-1} [ 1 - \sigma (hs_j) ] \cdot hs'_j $$

When update $ v_{w_I} $, we got gradient is:

$$ \nabla \log p(w | w_I) = \sum_{j=1}^{L(w)-1} [ 1 - \sigma (hs_j) ] \cdot [\![ n(w, j+1) = ch(n(w, j)) ]\!] \cdot v'_{n(w, j)} $$

When update $ v'_{n(w, j)}$ for each $j \in [1, L(w)-1]$, we got gradient is:

$$ \nabla \log p(w | w_I) = [ 1 - \sigma (hs_j) ] \cdot [\![ n(w, j+1) = ch(n(w, j)) ]\!] \cdot v_{w_I} $$

the negative sampling gradient¶

(2) $$ p(w_O | w_I) = \log \sigma( {v'_{w_O}}^T v_{w_I} ) + \sum_{i}^{k} E_{w_i \sim P_n(w)} \left[ \log \sigma( {-v'_{w_i}}^T v_{w_I} ) \right] $$

First let

$$ ns_{OI} = {v'_{w_O}}^T \cdot v_{w_I} $$$$ ns_{iI} = -{v'_{w_i}}^T \cdot v_{w_I} $$

And

the gradients $ns_{OI}$ according to ${v'_{w_O}}$ and $v_{w_I}$ are:

$$ \frac {d ns_{OI}} {v'_{w_O}} = v_{w_I} $$$$ \frac {d ns_{OI}} {v_{w_I}} = v'_{w_O} $$

the gradients $ns_{iI}$ according to ${v'_{w_i}}$ and $v_{w_I}$ are:

$$ \frac {d ns_{iI}} {v'_{w_i}} = -v_{w_I} $$$$ \frac {d ns_{iI}} {v_{w_I}} = -v'_{w_i} $$

Then

$$ \nabla p(w_O | w_I) = \frac {1} { \sigma(ns_{OI}) } \cdot \sigma'(ns_{OI}) \cdot ns'_{OI} + \sum_{i}^{k} E_{w_i \sim P_n(w)} \left[ \frac {1} { \sigma(ns_{iI}) } \cdot \sigma'( ns_{iI} ) \cdot ns'_{iI} \right] $$

According to equation (0)

$$ \nabla p(w_O | w_I) = \frac {1} { \sigma(ns_{OI}) } \cdot \sigma(ns_{OI}) \cdot [ 1 - \sigma(ns_{OI}) ] \cdot ns'_{OI} + \sum_{i}^{k} E_{w_i \sim P_n(w)} \left[ \frac {1} { \sigma(ns_{iI}) } \cdot \sigma ( ns_{iI} ) \cdot [ 1 - \sigma(ns_{iI}) ] \cdot ns'_{iI} \right] $$

simplify

$$ \nabla p(w_O | w_I) = [ 1 - \sigma(ns_{OI}) ] \cdot ns'_{OI} + \sum_{i}^{k} E_{w_i \sim P_n(w)} \left[ [ 1 - \sigma(ns_{iI}) ] \cdot ns'_{iI} \right] $$

When update $ v'_{w_O} $, we got gradient is:

$$ \nabla p(w_O | w_I) = [ 1 - \sigma(ns_{OI}) ] \cdot v_{w_I} $$

When update $ v_{w_I} $, we got gradient is:

$$ \nabla p(w_O | w_I) = [ 1 - \sigma(ns_{OI}) ] \cdot v'_{w_O} + \sum_{i}^{k} E_{w_i \sim P_n(w)} \left[ [ 1 - \sigma(ns_{iI}) ] \cdot -v'_{w_i} \right] $$

When update $ v'_{w_i} $, we got gradient is:

$$ \nabla p(w_O | w_I) = \sum_{i}^{k} E_{w_i \sim P_n(w)} \left[ [ 1 - \sigma(ns_{iI}) ] \cdot -v_{w_I} \right] $$

Finally the gradient of $ \log( p(w_O | w_I) )$ is:

$$ \nabla \log( p(w_O | w_I) ) = \frac{1} {p(w_O | w_I)} \cdot \nabla p(w_O | w_I) $$
